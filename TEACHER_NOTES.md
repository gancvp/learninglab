# Teacher Notes

This app teaches AI in a safe and simple way.

## Big picture

- AI is the broad idea of making computers act intelligently.
- Machine learning means the computer learns patterns from examples.
- Deep learning uses bigger neural-network models.
- Generative AI creates new text, code, images, or audio.

## What the student is learning here

- How data becomes inputs and correct answers.
- How probability and statistics help reason about uncertainty and data summaries.
- How a model makes a guess.
- How we measure mistakes with loss.
- How gradient descent slowly improves the model.
- Why neural networks are just layers of weighted calculations.
- Why GenAI systems need context, tools, and checks.
- Why SQL still matters because AI engineers often need to query and inspect data.
- Why programming interviews still test Python or C++ problem solving and debugging.
- Why modern AI apps often combine models with retrieval and tool use.

## Why the app uses local Ollama

- It keeps experiments private on the laptop.
- It reduces dependency on paid cloud APIs.
- It makes the system easier to inspect and understand.

## How to support the student

- Ask them to explain one screen in their own words.
- Ask them what the model input is, what the output is, and how error is measured.
- Ask them to explain mean, variance, CLT, Naive Bayes, and PCA in simple language.
- Ask them to compare plain prompting, retrieval, tool calling, and agents.
- Encourage them to build one mini-project and explain it aloud.

## Healthy expectations

- The student does not need advanced math to begin.
- They do need honest intuition about prediction, error, and optimization.
- The goal is not memorizing buzzwords. The goal is being able to explain and apply concepts clearly.
